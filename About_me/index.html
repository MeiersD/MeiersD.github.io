Hi, my name is Meiers Dixon. I am a Senior neuroscience major at Bucknell University. I also have minors in chemistry and computer science. Since my freshman year, I have been working in Dr. Robert Stockland's organic chemistry lab which has helped me find a passion for molecules and the structure of the world around us. Last year, I started to take more computer science courses after being inspired by the PyMol software we used in Biochemistry class. One of the reasons I like organic chemistry is because it turns something quantitative into a form of art. PyMol is very similar, it grants fantastic customization, control, and visualization of atoms in Protein Data Bank (PDB) files. This past summer, I worked in Dr. Scott Calabrese Barton's data science lab at Michigan State University. There, I created molecular dynamics simulations; using a software called GROMACS on the command line to create movies of proteins in real time. All of these experiences have led me to form a deep appreciation for the beauty of protein structures.
I hope you enjoyed the home page of my website. I am a big fan of PyMol, so I thought I'd try my hand at making a similar version of the software in javascript. I did not think I would end up liking javascript so much, but I'm finding it to be quite an enjoyable language. This entire website is hosted in a public repository on my Github, so please feel free to poke around there if you'd like. My roommates and I recently published a chrome extension called Eigofy, which helps Japanese people learn English. Please consider checking that out if you are interested.
